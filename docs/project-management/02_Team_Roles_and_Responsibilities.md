# Team Roles and Responsibilities

To keep the project organised, responsibilities were divided across the main areas of planning, development, testing, and documentation. A clear allocation of roles helped maintain accountability throughout the project and ensured that each stage of development received appropriate attention.

The Scrum Master and the Secretary took the lead in coordinating the work, monitoring progress, and helping the team remain on schedule. This included planning the sequence of activities, organising documentation, recording meetings, and reviewing progress as development continued. The Developers were mainly responsible for implementing the core scheduling logic, building the user interface, and integrating the main features of the application. The Tester focused on checking whether the system behaved correctly under normal, invalid, and edge-case conditions, while also helping to record issues that needed to be corrected.

Although roles were assigned separately, the project was managed flexibly so that team members could support one another where necessary. This made it easier to maintain progress, resolve technical issues, and ensure continuity throughout the development process.
